Datafication is a word that I’ve heard of before this class but I didn’t really know what it was. Datafication is the practice to collect and use people’s everyday digital media and technology habits. As Dr. Lindgren said in class, datafication is linked to issues of privacy, policy and commodification of people and their lives.

Before collecting data on ourselves and starting our Datastory jouney, Dr. Lindgren thought it was necessary for us to read articles surrounding ethical issues. The most basic definition of ethical issues is, a problem or situation that requires a person to choose between alternatives that must be evaluated as right or wrong. In the Beabout and Hannis reading, they mention how ethics is an art of character and it is something that a person just “has” based off their actions throughout their life. But not everyone follows the same ethical rules. When reading “Ethical Expediency: Classical Rhetoric, Technology and and the Holocaust” we see that the Nazi’s obviously did not have the same ethics as the most of the world. Katz brought up the term ‘ethics of expediency’ which he is referring to the convenience of something becomes more important than the ethical aspect of its purpose. In this document, he mentions how Hitler used the ethic of expediency rhetorically to create a moral justification for the Nazi actions. Ethical issues in research consists of ensuring the quality and integrity of what we are researching. Reviewing the terms ‘ethics’ and ‘ethics of expediency’ before collecting research on ourselves was important because it makes sure that we all follow the same ethical standards.

Data Collection

In order to fully understand the concept of Datafication, Dr. Lindgren designed a project that challenged us to learn how digital media and data are intertwined. This involved collecting data on ourselves for 5-7 days about our everyday digital habits. For my data collection, I decided to focus on the genre of music I listened to and how it affected my mood. I wanted to evaluate my mood on a numerical scale so I created my own scale from 1-5 and named it Influence. When the genre of music made my mood change for the better, I would rate it a 5; alternatively, if my mood got worse because of the genre of music, I would give it a 1. If the genre of music had no effect on my mood, I would rate it a 3. Whenever I listened to music within those 5 days straight I would record: the genre of music, what I was doing at that moment, my mood before, my modo after ,who picked the music and the influence the music had on changing my mood.

In quantitative research ethical standards prevents against fabrication or falsifying data. When doing our Data Collection project, we had to ensure we collected at least ten entries a day for five days. If for some reason we missed a day, we were able to continue to collect data throughout the weekend. It would’ve been easy to not collect data on ourselves but that wouldn’t have been very ethical of us.
